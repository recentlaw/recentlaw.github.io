Child Support

Everyone would agree that children have expensive needs and require support. However, the amount of support that should be paid is frequently an issue of intense debate. Although child support might seem obvious and simple to the casual observer, having a seasoned practitioner on your side can mean a substantial difference in your bank balance over time.

It is widely known that Illinois now uses income sharing models which serve as guidelines for the amount of support that should be paid, but not everyone understands that the court has the discretion under certain circumstances to order the obligor to pay more or less than the statutory guidelines. Even for the most straight forward case, it is important that your attorney take all factors into account on your behalf. How should bonus money, overtime, fluctuating income, or commissions be treated? What about the many tax issues at play? What about self-employment cases? And what about other expenses such as childcare, school tuition, health insurance premiums, uncovered medical expenses, and multiple family situations?

In additional to quickly and decisively establishing child support obligations that are fair to both those who pay and those who receive support, RCA attorneys have distinguished themselves by collecting an astonishing amount of support arrears from "deadbeat parents." RCA gets results because its attorneys have an intimate working knowledge of both family law and collection law, and the tools each area offers to maximize recovery by locating non-custodial parents, finding their assets, and using state and federal child support laws to ensure payment.

For many years, child support experts have turned to RCA when they needed help in Chicago. If you have child support issues, we urge you to take it seriously. We welcome your call.

Prenuptial Agreements

If failing to plan is truly planning to fail, then prenuptial agreements are a critical component of a successful marriage...and should the unexpected happen...a successful divorce.

While very few people exchange vows anticipating that a divorce is down the road, statistics tell us that nearly half of all first time marriages do in fact end in divorce. The numbers are even higher for subsequent marriages. Having a well thought out blueprint to follow during the marriage can only help provide stability and prevent arguments. For example, how during the marriage will you and your future spouse treat premarital assets, inheritance, marital income and expenses, and other assets such as real estate, financial investments, retirement and the like? Who will be expected to contribute to marital expenses and in what percentage? If one party owns the marital residence, who contributes to the mortgage and pays down the principal? And will it ever become marital property? Further, if you divorce, how will these same assets be treated upon dissolution of your marriage? What about spousal support? How will a family business be valued?

Second and third marriages add an even greater level of complexity to the situation, if there are children from other relationships that stand to inherit. In such a situation, you arguably have a responsibility to your children to make sure that the euphoria you feel today doesn’t leave them unprotected in the future.

Clearly, one needs only a marriage license to marry. Entering into the contract of marriage is easy, yet what comes after the "I do’s" is everyday life, which can be complicated. Even worse, divorce can be a nightmare. Having a good prenuptial agreement can avoid arguments, stress, and financial losses.

RCA has drafted many prenuptial agreements and takes pride in working with clients to mold each one to meet the client’s needs. If you are a responsible person and wish to discuss planning your future, we invite you to contact us.
